Career

My goal was to become a teacher. I wanted to teach secondary education and be an English Teacher either in Loudoun County or in Fairfax County. I wanted to get my master’s degree so I could also teach AP classes as well as dual enrollment. I would also like to teach summer school and become an adjunct part-time at a nearby community college. I think that teaching would be very fulfilling because I will get to influence future minds and give back to my community. Most recently, I accepted a position as an English teacher at a middle school nearby and I’m very excited to start my new job.

My grandmother used to say that she always knew I would be a teacher. She said she would always find me kneeling before a multitude of my stuffed animals as I taught them various subjects. At the young age of 7, I already has lesson plans. I can't remember when exactly I decided that I would teach, but it seems like it's always been a non negotiable for me. This is curious sometimes because I am a fairly nervous and shy person. I am not a very good public speaker.

When I got to college, I found the professor tenure track route appealing but always dreaded doing the research. I slowly began to realize that I didn't want to be a published author or famous scholar. I just wanted to return to the classroom because that's always where I felt at home. That is always where I did my best. A few months ago I received an email from the Loudoun Career fair. Not thinking of it I signed up for it and ended up receiving a job offer. I believe it was meant to be because although I didn't expect it to materialize this year, so fast. It did.

The cover letter that I used to obtain my position was this one.

Cover Letter:

Dear Hiring Team, I am interested in obtaining the available position as a 6-12 English teacher because reading and writing are my passion. Working with Loudoun County Public Schools would not only help me in advancing my career but it would also give me the opportunity of becoming more well versed in student/ teacher interactions as well as allowing me to give back to my community. Specifically, this position would give me the opportunity to share my knowledge with others and be able to discuss subjects in literature which I am passionate about. Working for LCPS would be an extremely exciting way of providing a crucial service to young kids and helping them shape a worldview where the humanities is a key part. Currently, I am attending my second to last semester of my master’s degree in English with a concentration in literature at George Mason University. During my time at George Mason, I have taken several courses towards my English major including Linguistics, Grammar, Synthesis, Fiction Writing, and various literature courses ranging from science fiction, to poetry, and even autobiography. Throughout my school years I have been thrust into different types of learning environments as well as varied content and genres, so I feel that I have the flexible mindset and mentality that will allow me to adapt to different educational needs. Prior to Mason, I graduated Magna Cum Laude with a bachelor’s degree in English from the University of South Florida in Tampa. In relation to my job experience, most recently I have been working as a Spanish interpreter for Lionbridge since I am a Native Spanish speaker. Lionbridge has provided me with a fastpaced working environment where multi-tasking and precision are essential. Every interpretation call is unique in that it can range from medical settings to court room hearings to healthcare applications, among many. I also have almost 9 years of customer service experience which makes me feel confident that I will be an asset to the county. Thank you for your time, Laura Chavez Medina
